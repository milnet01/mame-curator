## What was learned

- **Pydantic v2's validator wrapping is structural, not exception-chained.** A `model_validator(mode="after")` raising `ValueError` produces a `ValidationError` whose `errors()[0]['ctx']['error']` carries the original — `__cause__` is left `None`. The pre-FP06 docstring at `sessions.py:30` had this wrong; the misleading "with this `ValueError` as `__cause__`" claim survived FP05 because its tests didn't actually walk the exception chain. R1 fixed the docstring; B1a's structural assertion now pins the v2.x shape with both a loose-substring check and a strict structural check, so a future Pydantic patch-version drift surfaces visibly rather than silently invalidating the contract.
- **The under-scoped audit anti-pattern recurs.** B3 catalogued 10 path sites + 2 name sites = 12, but `sessions.py:81` (the `_active_must_reference_a_defined_session` validator's error message) was missed because the B3 audit grepped for `f"{path}"` and `f"'{name}'"` patterns separately — it didn't enumerate every f-string in the affected files. Closing review caught it via fresh-eyes pattern-matching ("if `name` flows from YAML, every f-string with `{...active...}` or `{...name...}` deserves audit"). R2 folded inline. The lesson for future FP##: audit by *value flow* (every interpolation of a user-controlled value), not by *grep pattern* (every line matching a regex).
- **Path-form-fragile assertions are easy to write and hard to spot.** The original B1b `assert repr(f) in msg or repr(str(f)) in msg` was satisfied both pre-fix and post-fix on a clean fixture path because `repr` of a clean string-path coincidentally produces the same single-quote characters that the bare interpolation would. Caught by closing-review M1; strengthened by R3 with a literal-LF fixture path. Test fixtures need a control byte to actually exercise the contract under test.
- **Pydantic version pin constraint logged.** B1/B2/R1/R2 reasoning depends on three Pydantic 2.x semantics. The project pins `pydantic>=2.10` with no upper bound; a future `pydantic>=3.0` resolve could silently break the "ValidationError IS-A ValueError" claim and thus the existing `pytest.raises((SessionsError, ValueError))` patterns. FP06 spec § "Constraint contract" logs this for the v3.0 upgrade window so it forces a fresh re-verification rather than a silent regression.
