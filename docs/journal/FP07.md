## What was learned

- **Single-point-of-change wins on rendering contracts.** A4's fix at `CopyError.__str__` covers ~7 raise sites in `recyclebin.py`, `playlist.py`, `executor.py` without per-site edits; A5's fix at `ParserError.__init__` covers similar raise sites across the parser module. Rule of Three's intent ("extract on the third call site") inverts here: when 5+ near-identical sites would *all* need the same one-line edit, fixing at the base class is *strictly better* than fanning out the edits — future raise sites inherit the fix automatically. Cost: one base-class change + one inheritance-aware test that loops every subclass. Benefit: zero ongoing maintenance.
- **Test assertion strength is a sliding scale, not a binary.** R1's lesson: `assert "\n" not in err.rstrip("\n")` is too strong — it asserts "no LF anywhere except trailing", which is a stronger claim than the FP07 contract requires. The right assertion is `assert "evil\nname.<ext>" not in err` — it directly tests the contract. The "no LF in trailing-stripped" form is *aspirational* (future `{exc}` might contain LFs); the literal-form check is *contractual*. Future closing reviews benefit from this distinction: when an assertion is "stronger than the contract", a future contract-stretching `{exc}` value breaks the test for the wrong reason.
- **`/indie-review`'s value is in catching surrounding-code findings.** FP07's audit lane was clean (CI gates pass; coverage holds); the indie-review lane found two findings — one fold-inline (R1) and one new fix-pass (FP08, `runner.py:233`). Without the indie-review lane, M2 would have shipped silently and surfaced months later as "why does my recycled-but-failed warning have a literal LF in it?". The dispatch-cost of running both lanes in parallel is ~3 minutes; the cost-savings of catching M2 here vs in production is unboundedly larger.
- **Spec line numbers drift from implementation.** Closing-review L1 noted `cli/__init__.py:139, 200, 249` in the spec drifted to `141, 203, 252` after implementation (FP06 → FP07 added line-shift). Cosmetic but worth flagging for future fix-passes: write spec citations at "current HEAD", and during implementation re-verify line numbers before quoting.
